Fly Fishing in Punta Arenas

Where silence, clarity, and the southern wind meet.

South of Punta Arenas, the landscape opens into endless steppe — a land where the sky dominates and the rivers carve quiet lines across the earth. Here, the experience of fly fishing becomes something deeper than the pursuit of fish; it becomes a return to simplicity and presence.

The spring creeks near Punta Arenas flow like veins of glass through low vegetation and golden grass. Their clarity challenges perception — every shadow, every ripple tells a story. Patience and delicacy become the true tools of the angler.

On the Río San Juan, the breeze carries the scent of salt and peat. The river meanders through wide valleys, its calm stretches perfect for sight fishing brown trout. It’s a place where time slows, where each cast feels like a quiet dialogue with the wind.

The Río del Medio offers intimate water — a setting for those who appreciate stealth and observation. Pools form behind scattered rocks, and every drift teaches something about precision and rhythm.

At Laguna Parrillar, reflections blend with clouds and mountain ridges. The stillness invites contemplation. Casts extend into silence, broken only by the soft rise of a trout under a mirrored surface.

Further west, the Río Penitente flows across open pampa, where light changes by the minute. It’s a river of contrasts — wide and strong yet tranquil in certain stretches — ideal for immersing yourself in the vastness of southern Patagonia.

Distances from Punta Arenas: between 50 and 90 km, depending on the river or lagoon selected.

Included: Bilingual guide, 4×4 transport, premium gear, waders, flies, and gourmet river lunch.

Each outing is designed not as a rush, but as an encounter — a day shaped by light, water, and the subtle grace of southern Patagonia. With Pristine Waters, every moment becomes a story told by the wind and remembered by the river.
