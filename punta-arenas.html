Punta Arenas — Fly Fishing at the Southern Edge

Rivers, spring creeks, and lagoons where wind, light, and trout write their own story.

Explore Waters

A Quiet Geography of Water

Just beyond the city limits, a network of intimate spring creeks, steady rivers, and reflective lagoons unfolds across open pampas and ancient forest patches. Each water has its rhythm — some reward delicate presentations and patience; others invite longer casts along cutbanks and gentle seams.

Here, fly fishing is elemental: the whisper of a line carried by southern wind, the soft step along grassy banks, the sudden pause of a trout beneath a sky that never seems to end. It’s a place to slow down, read the water, and feel the day expand.

Waters We Love

Spring Creeks

Clear, intimate flows that reward quiet steps and precise drifts. Subtle seams, grassy banks, and a light that changes everything.

Río San Juan

Diverse waters close to the city: gentle glides, deep turns, and long banks that invite a thoughtful approach with streamers or nymphs.

Río del Medio

A more intimate character — soft currents, clean structure, and readings that favor stealth, timing, and fine tippet control.

Laguna Parrillar

Quiet water bordered by native forest and wide skies. A place for unhurried casts, reflection, and the pleasure of light.

Río Penitente

A classic Magellanic river framed by open pampas and sweeping horizons. Wide pools and steady current define its calm, spacious rhythm.

Reading and Rhythm

From subtle spring-creek drifts to confident streamer swings, each water invites its own cadence — led by wind, light, and trout.
